Caspian Sea

The CASPIAN SEA (Caspian), the largest closed body of water on the globe, is a drainless brackish lake. Located on the southern border of Asia and Europe, it washes the shores of Russia, Kazakhstan, Turkmenistan, Iran and Azerbaijan. Due to the size, uniqueness of natural conditions and complexity of hydrological processes, the Caspian is usually classified as a closed inland seas. K. M. is located in a vast area of the interior. It drains and occupies a deep tectonic depression. The water level in the sea is at approx. 27 m below the World level approx., area approx. 390 thousand km2, volume approx. 78 thousand km3. The greatest depth is 1025 m. With a width of 200 to 400 km , the sea is stretched along the meridian by 1030 km .

Mangystau

Few people can remember from history textbooks that the Great Silk Road passed through the entire territory of the Mangystau region for 2000 years. And now this strategically important trade route has turned into an interesting tourist route. Mangystau abounds with unusually attractive landscapes combined with a variety of fauna of the Ustyurt Nature Reserve and unique natural monuments.
